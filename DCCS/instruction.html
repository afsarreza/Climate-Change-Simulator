The DCCS was inspired by generic dynamic stocks and flows tasks, and based on the simplified and adapted climate model. Detailed description of DCCS along with an experiment can be found in Dutt & Gonzalez (2008).

The figure given below shows a simulated climate change stabilization task that you will be playing today for a period of 100 years. The task shows a tank with grey colored liquid which represents the CO2 concentration in the atmosphere. The CO2 concentration can be read from graduations placed on the left tank wall,as well as below the tank.

The label on the left of the tank shows goals for CO2 concentration to be attained in the future. Also, the graphics at the left bottom shows the trajectory of total CO2 emissions and CO2 absorptions. You start in the year 2000 when the CO2 concentration in the atmosphere is 762 GtC (or billion tons of carbon) and total emissions equal 8.12 GtC/year.Also it contains a graphical representation of thermometer which shows the temperature of atmosphere in relation with concentration of carbon-dioxide in the current year. In year 2000, the value of CO2 absorptions was half of the total CO2 emissions and equaled 4.077 GtC/year and measured temperature is 25 degree celcious

We want you to estimate the future CO2 emissions and CO2 absorptions so that your CO2 concentration traces the goal trajectory. For accomplishing this, please enter the values of total CO2 emissions and CO2 absorptions in the corresponding text boxes . You can only enter total CO2 emissions and CO2 absorptions values that are within the FROM and TO ranges. Upon entering the total CO2 emissions and CO2 absorptions, you need to press the "Make Decision" button. This will make the task move forward in time by a year where the total CO2 emissions and absorptions you entered will come in and go out of the atmospheric tank in that year changing the CO2 concentration. The total CO2 emissions and absorptions you entered will also be plotted in the graphics below(where the blue line gives the total CO2 emissions and black line will give the CO2 absorptions you entered
